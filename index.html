Research Experience

  1. Describing wavefunction of nucleons inside a spherical nucleus

    Advisor: Dr. Achanta Venugopal, Tata Institute of Fundamental Research (TIFR), Mumbai

    (i) Solved and plotted wavefunction vs position for different values of principal quantum number.
    (ii) Found out the solutions of spherical bessel functions using Newton Raphson method and plotted them.
    (iii) The code can be modified for larger values of l and lower tolerances.




  2. Determining the impact of dielectric environment on graphene properties by Deep Learning

    Advisor: Prof. Radha Krishna & Dr. Gopi Krishna Guntupalli

    (i) Raman spectral data of graphene in different charge and dielectric environment are studied.
    (ii) Data augmentation was done through additive noise and peak shifting.
    (iii) CNN was implemented which showed 99% accuracy.




  3. Determining Critical temperature of superconductors using ML

    Advisor: Prof. Radha Krishna & Dr. Gopi Krishna Guntupalli

    (i) Analyzed the superconductivity dataset from the SuperCon database and plotted the correlation matrix to find that thermal conductivity, valency, density, etc. are the important features that determine the critical temperature of a superconductor.
    (ii) Analyzed metals which attain superconductivity at higher temperatures.
    (iii) Developed a ML algorithm that can predict the critical temperature of a superconductor by analysing these properties at room temperature.





  4. Review on usage of MXene based devices for Human Machine Interaction

    Advisor: Dr. Kaushik Parida, IIT Rookee

    (i) Contributed to writing a review on MXene based devices and how their utility can be enhanced by using ML techniques.
    (ii) Worked on piezoelectric and triboelectric devices and developed an algorithm that can classify different types of actions like tapping, folding and bending.


  5. WiFi indoor navigation using Quantum Machine Learning

    Advisor: Dr. Ahmed Farouk, Wilfrid Laurier University, Canada

    (i) Worked on quantum simulation of a CML algorithm using qiskit.
    (ii) The model could predict the individual's location inside a large building with the wifi access points sweeping the enitre area.
    (iii) These models can be very useful to predict the aftermath of the effect of natural disasters to buildings.




  6. Quantum Simulation of Graphene

    Advisor: Prof. Prashanta Kumar Panigrahi, IISER Kolkata

    (i) Worked on Variational Quantum Eigensolver algorithm and Qiskit Nature package
    (ii) Estimated the ground state and excited state energies for a graphene system.




  7. Methods of Preparation of graphene

    Advisor: Dr. Pranat Jain, IISER Berhampur

    (i) Did a reading project on several methods of preparation of graphene by top down and bottom up processes.
    (ii) Discussed several methods which can enhance the yield of the processes.



  8. Spam Detection using Quantum Tools

    Advisor: Prof. Prashanta Kumar Panigrahi, IISER Kolkata

    (i) With the help of IBMQ interface and a real world 5-qubit quantum computer, IBM X2, designed a Machine Learning algorithm to filter spam mails.
    (ii) The algorithm was targeted to perform to its utmost performance even in noisy environments.




  9. abrication of Microscale Metallic Contacts on Mechanically Exfoliated Graphene

    Advisor: Dr. Satyaprakash Sahoo, Institute Of Physics, Bhubaneswar

    (i) Mechanically exfoliated graphene by Scotch tape method
    (ii) TFabricated metallic contacts (Au, Ag and Al) on the exfoliated graphene and observed with a Photo Lithography system.
